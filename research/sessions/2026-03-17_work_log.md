### Meter-Based IDF Frequency Baseline
- Three frequency baselines implemented:
  1. **Full corpus** (default, 1,605 texts) — existing behavior
  2. **Same meter** (e.g., 214 hexameter texts) — deflates metrically conventional vocabulary
  3. **Text pair** (future, disabled)
- Backend: `_get_meter_doc_freqs()` queries inverted index restricted to meter group. Cached per meter. Falls back to corpus for non-Latin, unknown meter, or groups < 5 texts.
- Frontend: "Frequency Baseline" dropdown in Advanced Settings, visible only for Latin fusion searches
- Cache key includes `freq_basis` so switching triggers fresh search

### Single-Word Penalty Calibration for Meter IDF
- **Problem:** Meter IDF compressed score ranges ~5× (top multi-word pairs dropped from 19 to 4). The `SINGLE_WORD_PENALTY` (0.12) was calibrated for corpus IDF ranges and didn't provide enough separation — single-word matches became competitive with multi-word pairs (25/50 top results were single-word under meter IDF vs 2/50 under corpus).
- **Root cause:** Multi-word pairs deflated more than single-word pairs because they share more common hexameter vocabulary
- **Fix:** 3× tighter penalty when `freq_basis=meter` (effective penalty 0.036 vs 0.12, squared to 0.0013× vs 0.0144×)
- Structural fingerprint pairs (semantic + syntax_structural, zero shared vocabulary) correctly bypass the penalty by design

## Key Decisions

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Meter (not genre) for IDF | Formulaic vocabulary is metrically driven — "Neptunia proles" recurs because it fits hexameter, not because it's specifically epic vs didactic. Genre subdivisions too small for stable IDF. |
| Era from author_dates.json | Don't reinvent existing metadata. 14 eras already defined for 247 Latin authors. |
| Didactic as separate genre | Lucretius DRN, Manilius, Vergil Georgics — distinct from epic for scholarly purposes even though same meter |
| Lock dir project-local | Apache PrivateTmp=yes hides /tmp from us. Project-local dir always visible. |
| Tighter single-word penalty for meter IDF | Score compression makes 0.12 penalty insufficient. 0.036 restores separation. |
