# Tesserae V6 Synonym Dictionary Additions

## Overview

This document describes the V6 synonym dictionary additions made on 2026-03-06.
These additions expand the V3 synonym dictionaries (derived from Lewis & Short
and LSJ lexicon definitions by University at Buffalo graduate students) with
curated vocabulary entries targeting poetic synonym substitutions (*variatio*)
underrepresented in the lexicographic source material.

## Provenance

- **V3 Originals:** Preserved in `originals_v3/` with checksums
- **V6 Additions:** Generated by Claude (Anthropic) in collaboration with
  Neil Coffee, informed by classical lexicography and attested patterns of
  poetic substitution in Latin and Greek epic, didactic, and dramatic poetry
- **Addition files:** `v6_additions/latin_v6_additions.csv`,
  `v6_additions/greek_v6_additions.csv`, `v6_additions/greek_latin_v6_additions.csv`
- **Working files:** `fixed_latin_syn.csv`, `fixed_greek_syn.csv`,
  `greek_latin_dict.csv` (originals + additions appended)

## Methodology

Entries were selected based on the following criteria:

1. **Attested substitution:** The word pair or group represents a substitution
   pattern actually used by Latin or Greek poets (e.g., *corrumpo* for *vasto*
   in Vergil/Lucretius plague narratives).

2. **Semantic precision:** Groups are kept small (typically 2–5 words) to avoid
   false positive matches. Words are grouped only if they share a core meaning
   that would make one a plausible substitute for the other in a poetic context.

3. **Gap-filling:** Priority was given to semantic fields poorly covered by the
   V3 dictionaries, particularly:
   - **Verbs:** The V3 dictionary is derived from noun-oriented lexicon definitions
     and has sparse verb coverage. V6 adds systematic coverage of verbs of
     destruction, motion, speech, perception, emotion, and physical action.
   - **Plague vocabulary:** The Thomas (1988) Georgics–DRN case study exposed
     specific gaps (*corrumpo/vasto*, *inficio/exhaurio*) that are now covered.
   - **Cross-lingual verbs:** The V3 Greek-Latin dictionary is strongest on
     nouns and weakest on verbs. V6 adds ~120 verbal cross-lingual entries.

4. **Headword forms:** All entries use standard dictionary headwords (first
   person singular present active for Latin verbs, present active infinitive
   or first person singular for Greek verbs, nominative singular for nouns).
   The matching engine normalizes to lowercase; Greek entries are further
   normalized by stripping diacritics.

### Greek Intra-Language (27 fields)

Organized by the same semantic categories, with emphasis on Homeric and
tragic vocabulary including rare poetic forms (e.g., *κίω, σεύομαι, λεύσσω,
δέρκομαι, φάσγανον, ἄορ*).

### Greek-Latin Cross-Lingual (36 fields)

Each entry maps Greek lemma(s) to Latin translation equivalents, focusing on
the vocabulary of epic poetry where Greek-to-Latin allusion is most common.
Coverage extends to verbs, nouns, adjectives, and key cultural/religious
terminology. Special attention to plague vocabulary (field 36) supporting
Thucydides–Lucretius–Vergil intertextuality research.

## Verification

The additions can be verified against:
- Lewis & Short *Latin Dictionary* for Latin entries
- Liddell-Scott-Jones *Greek-English Lexicon* for Greek entries
- Montanari *GE* for Greek-Latin equivalences
- Standard commentaries (e.g., Austin, Thomas, Horsfall) for poetic substitution patterns

## Usage

The working CSV files are loaded automatically by `backend/synonym_dict.py`.
No code changes are needed — the existing `_load_v3_synonym_file()` function
reads the appended entries. The dictionary channel in fusion search will
immediately benefit from the expanded vocabulary.
